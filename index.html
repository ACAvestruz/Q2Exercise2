Science 9: First and Second Quarter

Impulse

Impulse is the product of the net force acting on an object and the time interval during which the force is applied.

  • vector quantity (has magnitude and direction)
  • direction: same as the net force (F)
  • SI unit: Ns

Formula

J = Ft

J is impulse (Ns), F is Force (N), and t is time (s)

Momentum

Momentum is the product of the mass of an object and its velocity. The "quantity of motion"

  • vector quantity (has magnitude and direction)
  • direction: same as the velocity (v)
  • SI unit: kgm/s

Formula

p = mv

p is momentum (kgm/s), m is mass (kg), v is velocity (m/s)

Valence Electrons

Valence electrons are found on the most outermost shell of an electron.

  • can be determined using the groups (vertical) of a periodic table (for Group A)
  • Octet Rule: states that elements with 8 valence electrons are stable (example: Group 8A)
    • elements bond to achieve the octet rule (stability)

    Examples

    Florine (F) - 7 valence electrons
